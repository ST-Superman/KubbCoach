## Inkasting/Drilling Tips

### The Fundamentals of Drilling

**Quote:** "The invention of drilling (I and Tord Ahgren invented that in 1996 and we were alone with it for 4-5 years)"

- **Source:** Terry Ekelöf, Team Ekeby (10-time World Champions)
- **PDF:** 2011 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** Interview section, pages 6-7
- **Summary:** Drilling was invented in 1996 and revolutionized kubb. It's the technique of throwing kubbs with counterclockwise rotation (for right-handers) to control where they land after bouncing.
- **Category:** Trivia

---

**Quote:** "The inkastare tosses the kubb underarm and puts counterclockwise rotation (if you are right-handed) on the kubb. Think about throwing a football and putting a tight spin on the ball. That is the kubb, except it is underarm and the rotation is the opposite direction. The object is to get the spinning kubb to hit the ground in a way so that it lands with one of the points digging into the ground, just like a drill."

- **Source:** General technique description
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 25-26
- **Summary:** Basic drill technique involves underarm throwing with counterclockwise spin (for righties), aiming to land the kubb on a point so it digs into the ground like a drill bit.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "When drilling a kubb, don't land the shuttle, you want to crash the plane."

- **Source:** Josh Feathers, 2011 U.S. Champion (Knockerheads)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** The angle of release is critical - you want the kubb to hit the ground at a steep angle (crash the plane) rather than a shallow angle. This advice dramatically improves drilling technique.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "You need at least a stable fly. You have to rotate the kubb like a sparrow. The javelin guys do it too, because they want to have stable fly. So it's the same for us. The rotation comes not from the arm or the hand. For me its only one digit. The longest one. I put this finger under the kubb and then I move from bottom to top."

- **Source:** Björn Görlitz, Fortschritt99 (2011 European Champions)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 27
- **Summary:** Rotation should come from a single finger (middle finger) moving from bottom to top under the kubb, not from the whole hand or arm. This creates stable flight.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "I switched to drilling because it allows us to control the entire phase of our turn during a game, not just the part where we throw batons. Previous to drilling, throwing out field kubbs was like a controlled crash and seemed entirely out of our hands... Drilling gives our team much greater control of where the kubbs land."

- **Source:** Eric Goplin, Tad Kubbler (3-time top-5 U.S. Championship finisher)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 27
- **Summary:** Drilling transforms kubb throwing from random chance to controlled precision, giving you control over the entire turn, not just the baton-throwing phase.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

### Building the Wall (Front Right Corner Strategy)

**Quote:** "If you can raise a kubb in, then it must be raised in. Therefore, if a kubb is on the ground and part of it is in and part of it is out-of-bounds, the defending team is required to raise it in. If you can put one or more kubbs on either the mid-pitch line and/or the sideline, those kubbs will be raised closer together, as any option to raise the kubb away from other kubbs is eliminated."

- **Source:** General strategy explanation
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 27
- **Summary:** The right front corner strategy works because kubbs on the boundary lines must be raised in-bounds, eliminating the opponent's options for spreading them out.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "My starting drill should lie exactly on this point where the kubb touched the green. No roll to right or left. I try to hit the right line in the right corner. For this you have to change the rotation to very low. The kubb should fly stable, but with less rotation. Then I normally use the right spin drill. The kubb jumps to the right and will be stopped from the first one. So all kubbs are really close together."

- **Source:** Björn Görlitz, Fortschritt99 (2011 European Champions)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** First kubb should have minimal rotation and land exactly on the corner line. Subsequent kubbs use right-spin to bounce into the first kubb, building a tight wall.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "I try to drill the first kubb(s) with no movement. Then, the following kubbs would roll to the right after they hit the ground."

- **Source:** Scott Forster, team Default
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** Build the wall by placing the first kubb(s) stationary against the line, then bounce subsequent kubbs into them from the right.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "When throwing a large pile of blocks, my first kubb I will try to place it ON the centerline, close to the pin, so that it is forced in bounds. I then try to 'skip' my kubbs in right behind it. I feel that depth in the court kubbs is more important than having the kubbs close to the centerline. My first kubb pretty much just acts as a marker for my other throws, and acts as a cushion to kubbs that would otherwise roll out of bounds."

- **Source:** Josh Feathers, 2011 U.S. Champion (Knockerheads)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** Place first kubb as a marker and backstop on the centerline, then skip remaining kubbs behind it. Depth is more important than proximity to centerline.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "If I have to throw more than three kubbs I will try to get the first ones in a good position in the corner, and I will try to bounce the rest on to the first ones. The strategy varies slightly depending on the surface."

- **Source:** Niklas Ahlgren, Elefantbajs 2000 (2006 World Champions, 2x runners-up)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** With 3+ kubbs, establish corner position first, then bounce remaining kubbs onto the initial group. Adjust for surface conditions.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

### Bounce Control Techniques

**Quote:** "For right-handed players, if you angle the kubb so that the front end that is travelling is more to the left and the rear (facing you) is to the right, and if you bring it down at the correct angle, the kubb will bounce to the right. How much to the right? That depends on the angle from left to right, the angle that it hits the ground, force you are throwing it, and some believe the amount of spin you put on the kubb."

- **Source:** General technique explanation
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** Control bounce direction by angling the kubb in mid-air. Left-forward/right-back creates right bounce. Multiple factors affect distance: angle, force, spin, and ground angle.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "If I would like to place the kubb close behind the kubbs already lying on the field I dip the front end and angle the kubb towards the ground and throw the kubb looser than normally."

- **Source:** Joakim Ekelöf, Team Ekeby (considered by many as best kubb player in world)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** For placing kubbs behind existing ones (backing them up), dip the front end down and throw softer than usual.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "My basic strategy is to get the kubb to move from left to right after it hits the ground. To make that happen I need to spin the kubb and throw it with the right angle. I turn my hand and wrist in the same angle as I want it to land with."

- **Source:** Marcus Hansson, Team Ekeby (2011 World Championship inkastare)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** Match your hand/wrist angle to the desired landing angle of the kubb to achieve left-to-right movement after bounce.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "The bounce drill throw is not the ultimate inkast. The best would be to throw them without a bounce. I don't know if that is possible at all ground conditions - certainly not in Rone where it almost always is kind of dry and hard in the ground. In wet or damp-soft ground it is possible to throw them so they stick where they land."

- **Source:** Terry Ekelöf, Team Ekeby (9-time World Champion)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28-29
- **Summary:** The ultimate drill is no-bounce - kubbs stick where they land. Only possible in wet/damp-soft conditions. On hard ground like Rone, bounce drills are necessary.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "Björn said he has five types of throws."

- **Source:** Björn Görlitz, Fortschritt99 (2011 European Champions)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 28
- **Summary:** Elite inkastare develop multiple throw types to handle different situations and field conditions.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

### Measuring Success

**Quote:** "I am happy when my opponent has no options for kubb placement. Taking away the only defense your opponent has against field kubbs is a significant advantage. Generally, your opponents choose which end to stand a kubb on, unless you have a nice group with kubbs on the line, and huddled up together in the corner. I will generally watch the opponent right the kubbs, not to make sure they are following the rules, but more to count the kubbs they were able to make a decision on. I have found that the lower the options I give an opponent, the higher amount of kubbs our team can take down."

- **Source:** Josh Feathers, 2011 U.S. Champion (Knockerheads)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 29
- **Summary:** Success can be measured by eliminating opponent options. Count how many kubbs they can't choose the orientation for - the fewer options you give, the more kubbs your team will knock down.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "Best case is, if the person who turns up the kubbs can't do anything. The kubbs are on the lines or really close to them and they can't choose the alignment direction."

- **Source:** Björn Görlitz, Fortschritt99 (2011 European Champions)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 29
- **Summary:** Perfect drill: opponent has zero choice in how to orient the kubbs because they're on/near the lines.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

---

**Quote:** "I am happy with my performance if I have placed the kubbs so that there is a realistic chance one stick could knock them all down. And, of course, I am never happy with a loss."

- **Source:** Niklas Ahlgren, Elefantbajs 2000 (2006 World Champions)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 29
- **Summary:** Goal is to group kubbs tightly enough that one baton has a realistic shot at knocking them all down.
- **Category:** Inkasting/drilling

### The Future of Drilling

**Quote:** "I think there will be more and more people doing the drill technique really well. That will raise the overall standard of play at the World Championship. If there is only one person on your team who knows the technique you could lose a game to a lesser opponent if that kubb tosser, at the moment, is playing badly."

- **Source:** Niklas Ahlgren, Elefantbajs 2000 (2006 World Champions)
- **PDF:** 2012 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** "The Drill" article, page 31
- **Summary:** Future teams will need multiple inkastare as insurance. Relying on one person creates vulnerability.

- **Source:** Aaron Ellringer, The Ringers (2010 U.S. Champions)
- **PDF:** 2016 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** Ringers interview section, page 18

- **PDF:** 2016 Kubbnation Magazine

## 4-Meter/Blasting Tips

**Quote:** "I am convinced that, in the future, the best teams need to master throwing the 'shorts' as far away as possible with a consistent hit rate knocking them down. This is because you will need to minimize the penalty for missing a short one and the benefit of taking out all the 'shorts' together with one from the base line. We have been talking about initializing some serious training some day to make that real. It is, however, quite a lot more difficult."

- **Source:** Terry Ekelöf, Team Ekeby (9-time World Champion)
- **PDF:** 2011 Kubbnation Magazine
- **Page Reference:** Interview section, page 7
- **Summary:** The future of elite kubb is mastering long-distance shots on field kubbs placed far back. This allows taking out shorts + baseline kubbs together and minimizes penalty for missing. Very difficult skill requiring serious training.
- **Category:** 4m/blasting

### Key Concepts

1. **The Drill** - Revolutionary inkasting technique invented 1996 by Terry Ekelöf and Tord Ahgren
2. **Front Right Corner Strategy** - Drilling kubbs to front right corner to eliminate opponent options
3. **Building the Wall** - Creating a backstop of kubbs along the sideline
4. **The 10,000 Hour Rule** - Elite inkasting requires approximately 10,000 hours of practice
5. **Crash the Plane** - Steep angle drilling technique for better control
6. **90-10 Rule** - 90% of game happens in 10% of field (the corner)
7. **Play Not to Lose** - Elite mindset focuses on minimizing mistakes over maximizing performance
8. **Persistent Performance** - Consistency matters more than maximum performance
