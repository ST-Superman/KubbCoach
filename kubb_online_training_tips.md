# Comprehensive Kubb Training Tips and Strategies

A comprehensive collection of training tips, techniques, and strategies for kubb organized by category. All tips include source attribution.

---

## 8-Meter Throws (Baseline Throwing)

### Throwing Technique

1. **180-Degree Rotation**
   - Always try to make the throwing stick rotate 180 degrees when throwing, which has been proven the best way
   - Aim for consistency - some players use 180-degree rotation while others use a full 360-degree rotation, perfecting one style allows you to become very accurate
   - [Source: Kubb Tips, Strategies, and Rules](https://www.tyrstrekubb.com/tips-rules)
   - [Source: How to Play Kubb with Simple Rules and Setup](https://tosscornhole.com/how-to-play-kubb/)

2. **Lock Your Elbow**
   - Locking your elbow makes it easier to aim and improves overall accuracy
   - Keep your arm steady and aim carefully toward your target
   - [Source: Kubb Tips, Strategies, and Rules](https://www.tyrstrekubb.com/tips-rules)
   - [Source: How To Throw Kubb](https://storables.com/gardening-and-outdoor/outdoor-recreation-and-activities/how-to-throw-kubb/)

3. **Underhand Throwing Motion**
   - Batons must always be thrown underhanded with a smooth release
   - Each throw must be underhand, with the baton end pointing in the direction of flight
   - Your throwing motion should make the baton rotate end over end vertically
   - [Source: How To Throw Kubb](https://storables.com/gardening-and-outdoor/outdoor-recreation-and-activities/how-to-throw-kubb/)
   - [Source: Rules in the WC](https://www.kubbvm.com/en/about-the-kubb-world-championship/rules-in-the-wc/)

4. **Vertical Rotation Only**
   - Batons must rotate within 30-45 degrees of vertical when thrown (depending on tournament rules)
   - Cannot spin the batons horizontally like a helicopter during baseline throws
   - Most beginners find vertical rotation easier to control
   - [Source: Rules of Kubb — Kubb United](http://www.kubbunited.com/rules-of-kubb)
   - [Source: How to Play Kubb: A Beginner's Guide](https://freshhobby.com/how-to-play-kubb/)

### Grip and Stance

1. **Comfortable Grip**
   - Grip the baton comfortably but securely — too tight can reduce accuracy, too loose might affect control
   - Experiment with different grips on the baton to find what feels best
   - Hold it near one end and make sure your grip is comfortable and your arm is relaxed
   - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)
   - [Source: How To Throw Kubb](https://storables.com/gardening-and-outdoor/outdoor-recreation-and-activities/how-to-throw-kubb/)

2. **Proper Stance**
   - Stand firmly with your feet shoulder-width apart behind your baseline for balance
   - Both feet must be completely behind the current throwing line and inside the side lines at the start of the throw
   - Keep both feet behind your throwing line and do not step over it during or after your throw
   - [Source: How To Throw Kubb](https://storables.com/gardening-and-outdoor/outdoor-recreation-and-activities/how-to-throw-kubb/)
   - [Source: How to Play Kubb: A Beginner's Guide](https://freshhobby.com/how-to-play-kubb/)

3. **Footwork and Balance**
   - No step may be taken forward over the throwing line or past the side line at all
   - Maintain balance during the throw and focus on striking the center of the kubb
   - Jumping and throwing is not allowed
   - [Source: Rules in the WC](https://www.kubbvm.com/en/about-the-kubb-world-championship/rules-in-the-wc/)
   - [Source: Kubb Tips, Strategies, and Rules](https://www.tyrstrekubb.com/tips-rules)

4. **Perpendicular Positioning**
   - Always try to stand so that you are perpendicular to the kubb (flat side facing you)
   - This positioning makes your chance of impact greatest
   - [Source: 5 Kubb-tips](https://www.kubb.info/5-tips-to-always-win-with-kubb/)

### Release and Trajectory

1. **Smooth Release**
   - Practice releasing the baton at the right moment to achieve optimal trajectory and spin
   - Focus on a smooth, controlled release rather than forcing the throw
   - [Source: How To Throw Kubb](https://storables.com/gardening-and-outdoor/outdoor-recreation-and-activities/how-to-throw-kubb/)

2. **Consistency Over Force**
    - Aim for consistency over force — the goal is to have enough momentum to knock the kubb down without launching the baton into the next postcode
    - It is more important to use less power and be accurate than to use power and completely miss
    - [Source: Kubb Set: How to Play the Classic Game and Win](https://trans4mind.com/counterpoint/index-health-fitness/kubb-set-play-classic-game-and-win.html)
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Throwing Too Hard**
    - The most important key to blasting is not missing — at all costs. Everyone misses, but limiting these by not throwing too hard is critical
    - A complete miss is the absolute worst thing that can happen because it can make you anxious
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

2. **Inconsistent Technique**
    - It is important to not change your technique, keep throwing in the same way, even if it does not go well in the beginning
    - It's more important to learn a consistent spiral and then tweak your throw until you "perfect" it instead of trying to be exactly like someone else
    - [Source: Kubb Tips, Strategies, and Rules](https://www.tyrstrekubb.com/tips-rules)
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

3. **Poor Contact Point**
    - Throwing the baton a little high causes the baton to skip off the top of the kubb
    - Hitting the kubb square with the end of the baton and too low, with the baton having no action and going nowhere after contact, leads to disappointing results
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

4. **Inaccurate Throwing Technique**
    - One common mistake is underestimating the importance of accurate throwing techniques
    - A misjudged or imprecise throw can easily lead to missed targets and wasted opportunities
    - [Source: Kubb Game and Rules Guide](https://funattic.com/kubb-game-and-rules-guide/)

### Practice Drills

1. **Corner Pin Accuracy Drill**
    - Rather than throwing at kubbs during 8-meter practice, throw at corner pins instead
    - Corner pins are much narrower than a kubb, so hitting a corner pin two to three times out of six throws indicates good accuracy
    - [Source: How I Practice: Kyle Weakland](https://kubbon.com/2018/07/23/how-i-practice-kyle-weakland/)

2. **Multi-Distance Training**
    - Set up six corner pins spread at different distances (typically one at 4 meters and 8 meters with four others at various distances in between)
    - Use six batons to try and hit each pin once
    - This helps with general accuracy and in-between accuracy (about 5 to 7 meters)
    - [Source: How I Practice: Kyle Weakland](https://kubbon.com/2018/07/23/how-i-practice-kyle-weakland/)

3. **Solo Practice Field**
    - Set up a solo practice field to get familiar with the weight of the batons, the trajectory of your throw, and the way kubbs react when hit
    - If you can hit a single kubb on command, you're golden
    - [Source: Kubb Set: How to Play the Classic Game and Win](https://trans4mind.com/counterpoint/index-health-fitness/kubb-set-play-classic-game-and-win.html)

4. **Make Training Harder Than Competition**
    - To push yourself to the next level, it's important to make practice more difficult than the normal game
    - Developing games that mimic kubb situations can help you get better as an overall player
    - [Source: How I Practice: Kyle Weakland](https://kubbon.com/2018/07/23/how-i-practice-kyle-weakland/)

5. **Practice Baton Juggling**
    - Some people practice juggling the batons to perfect the spin when tossing the baton in gameplay
    - This technique improves the rotation and proper release of the baton
    - [Source: Rules of Kubb — Kubb United](http://www.kubbunited.com/rules-of-kubb)

### Strategy and Mental Game

1. **Target Selection**
    - Always try to stand perpendicular to the kubb (flat side facing you) for greatest chance of impact
    - A good strategy is to aim at the nearest kubbs first - so that if any field kubbs are not toppled, at least the opponents will be as far away as possible
    - [Source: 5 Kubb-tips](https://www.kubb.info/5-tips-to-always-win-with-kubb/)
    - [Source: How to Play Kubb: A Beginner's Guide](https://freshhobby.com/how-to-play-kubb/)

2. **Watch and Learn**
    - Watch your opponent's technique and learn from good throwers by noticing their grip, release point, and follow through
    - Playing against different opponents exposes you to different tactics and strategies
    - [Source: Kubb Strategies and Tips](https://kangjiegardengame.com/kubb-strategies-and-tips-wanna-crush-your-opponents/)

3. **Save Your Best Thrower**
    - If you need one perfect shot to win, let your most accurate player take it
    - Save your best thrower for last in critical moments
    - [Source: Kubb Game and Rules Guide](https://funattic.com/kubb-game-and-rules-guide/)

## Blasting / 4-Meter Throws (Field Kubb Throwing)

### Blasting Fundamentals

1. **Three Key Points of Blasting**
    - Successful blasts depend on three key points: power, rotation, and placement/alignment
    - Each of these are important to a successful blast
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

2. **Power vs Finesse Decision**
    - When blasting a tight pile, power is the most important aspect
    - With a loose pile, finesse is more important
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

3. **Finesse Approach**
    - Throw with light to medium power
    - It is much more important to hit one kubb than to try and take out two or three and miss completely
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

4. **Hit the Top Corner**
    - Hitting the top front corner of the kubb with the baton about halfway between vertical and horizontal helps the baton create more chaos
    - The baton tumbles straight over top of the kubb and spins off to one side or the other, creating collateral damage
    - By hitting the top corner, you hit it at its weakest spot
    - The lower to the ground you hit the kubb the harder you need to hit it to make it go anywhere
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

5. **Control Over Power**
    - Some players mistakenly believe that force is the key to victory, focusing solely on powerful throws
    - It is more important to use less power and be accurate than to use power and completely miss
    - [Source: Kubb Game and Rules Guide](https://funattic.com/kubb-game-and-rules-guide/)

### Blasting Practice Drills

1. **London Bridge Drill**
    - At the 4 meter mark (mid-field) three kubbs are stacked like London bridge: two vertically and one horizontal on top
    - Work on throwing a baton softly and low with the right rotation to slide it through London Bridge and hit the kubb down
    - [Source: Skill Builder Wednesday: Week One](https://kubbon.com/2019/04/17/skill-builder-wednesday-week-one/)

2. **Short Porch Challenges**
    - Work on loose piles and the 2 and 3 meter distances
    - Practice for quarter, half, and full baton rotations
    - [Source: Skill Builder Wednesday: Week Eight](https://kubbon.com/2019/06/05/skill-builder-wednesday-week-eight/)

3. **6 and 7 Meter Distance Training**
    - Practice at the 6 and 7 meter distances that don't often get played
    - These intermediate distances are common in actual games but often neglected in practice
    - [Source: Skill Builder Wednesday: Week Eight](https://kubbon.com/2019/06/05/skill-builder-wednesday-week-eight/)

4. **3-4-3 Drill**
    - Focus on inkasting three or four kubbs into a pile, then try to take them out
    - This is important because you'll see piles of three to four kubbs more often in a match than eight, nine or 10
    - Developing the skill to take out three to four kubbs in one to two batons early can be the difference between winning and losing
    - [Source: How I Practice: Kyle Weakland](https://kubbon.com/2018/07/23/how-i-practice-kyle-weakland/)

### Blasting Strategy

1. **The Blaster Role**
    - The blaster is often the first player to throw at a pile of field kubbs
    - Their specialty is knocking down multiple kubbs at a time
    - [Source: Skill Builder Wednesday: Week One](https://kubbon.com/2019/04/17/skill-builder-wednesday-week-one/)

2. **Accuracy is Critical**
    - The most important key to blasting is not missing — at all costs
    - Limiting misses by not throwing too hard is critical
    - A complete miss can make you anxious and cause you to try too much with your next baton, snowballing into a disastrous round
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

3. **Use Advantage Line Effectively**
    - If you get an advantage line, use it wisely
    - Knock down as many baseline kubbs as possible before your opponent can recover
    - The advantage line makes hitting baseline kubbs much easier
    - [Source: Mastering Kubb: The Ultimate Guide to Winning Strategies](https://elakaioutdoor.com/blogs/lifestyle/mastering-kubb-the-ultimate-guide-to-winning-strategies)

4. **Understand Rotation Requirements**
    - Practice with different rotation amounts (quarter, half, full) at different distances
    - Successful blasts require proper rotation matched to the distance
    - [Source: Skill Builder Wednesday: Week Eight](https://kubbon.com/2019/06/05/skill-builder-wednesday-week-eight/)

### Mental Approach to Blasting

1. **Don't Force It**
    - Some players mistakenly believe force is key to victory
    - Consider strategic aspects rather than focusing solely on powerful throws
    - [Source: Kubb Game and Rules Guide](https://funattic.com/kubb-game-and-rules-guide/)

2. **Limit Anxiety After Misses**
    - A complete miss can make you anxious
    - You might try to do too much with your next baton
    - Stay calm and stick to your technique
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

## Inkasting / Drilling (Throwing Kubbs Back)

### Drilling Fundamentals

1. **What is Drilling?**
    - Drilling is a specific kubb throwing technique employed to strategically place field kubbs so they land very closely together
    - The player performing the majority of kubb throwing strategy is called the driller (or inkastare)
    - This is one of the most important roles on a team
    - [Source: Drilling – Planet Kubb](https://planetkubb.com/post/drilling/)
    - [Source: New to Kubb: Team Roles](https://kubbon.com/2017/06/28/new-kubb-team-roles/)

2. **Two Key Principles**
    - The kubb is spun on its center axis, similar to a spin of a thrown football
    - Unlike a football, the nose of the kubb is generally pointed downward during the throwing motion
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)
    - [Source: Drilling – Planet Kubb](https://planetkubb.com/post/drilling/)

3. **"Crash the Plane, Don't Land It"**
    - A famous line in the kubb community: "you want to crash the plane, not land it"
    - This means you want the "nose" of the kubb to contact the ground
    - The angle at which the kubb hits the ground is very important
    - You don't want the plane to hit too vertically or it will tumble end over end
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

### Drilling Technique

1. **Softer Touch for Control**
    - A softer touch is going to be easier to control as you start
    - Make sure you're not throwing too hard
    - Make sure you're not trying to spin the kubb too hard
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

2. **Find Your Spin Happy Medium**
    - Spin too hard and it can be hard to control and inconsistent
    - Spin too little and the kubb won't stay straight
    - Make sure you find your happy medium
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

3. **Proper Nose Angle**
    - The nose of the kubb should be pointed downward during the throwing motion
    - You want the "nose" to contact the ground, not land flat
    - Don't hit too vertically or the kubb will tumble end over end
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

4. **Football-Style Spin**
    - The kubb is spun on its center axis, similar to a thrown football
    - This spiral motion keeps the kubb stable in flight
    - [Source: Drilling – Planet Kubb](https://planetkubb.com/post/drilling/)

5. **Spin Control**
    - Aim for the kubbs with a gentle spin to increase accuracy and stability upon impact
    - A softer touch is going to be easier to control as you start
    - Make sure you're not throwing too hard and not trying to spin the kubb too hard
    - [Source: How To Throw Kubb](https://storables.com/gardening-and-outdoor/outdoor-recreation-and-activities/how-to-throw-kubb/)
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

6. **Two-Step Learning Process**
    - Step 1: Become good at throwing the drill "spiral" and hitting your spot you are aiming at
    - Step 2: Learn how to "cut" the kubb so they land on the ground and move to the spot you want them to end up
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

### Drilling Practice

1. **Time Investment Required**
    - It took one experienced player about two months of training a few times a week before achieving consistency with drilling
    - Beginners should never be afraid to go back and practice at a shorter distance until they get it
    - Work their way back up gradually
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

2. **Learn Consistent Spiral First**
    - It's more important to learn a consistent spiral and then tweak your throw until you "perfect" it
    - Don't try to be exactly like someone else
    - Develop your own consistent technique
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

3. **Practice at Shorter Distances**
    - Beginners should never be afraid to go back and practice at a shorter distance until they get it
    - Once consistent at shorter distances, work your way back up to full 8 meters
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

4. **Maintain Consistency**
    - It is important to not change your technique, keep throwing in the same way
    - Even if it does not go well in the beginning, stick with your approach
    - [Source: Kubb Tips, Strategies, and Rules](https://www.tyrstrekubb.com/tips-rules)

### Strategic Inkasting

1. **Cluster Strategy**
    - When throwing knocked-over kubbs into your opponent's field, aim to cluster them together for easier targeting
    - Adopting a cluster strategy by aiming to get field kubbs to land close together by throwing them in a high arc
    - Grouped kubbs are easier to hit multiple times with one baton
    - [Source: How to Play Kubb – Black Label Billiards](https://blacklabelbilliards.com/blogs/blog/how-to-play-kubb)
    - [Source: Kubb Game: A Complete Guide](https://threostore.com/blog/kubb-game-the-unique-swedish-throwing-game/)

2. **Deep Inkasting Strategy**
    - Inkasting a kubb far into the upfield near the opponent's baseline is generally done with intention of setting up a possible double with a base kubb
    - Can also be used as a conservative move if the inkastare believes they have more kubbs to throw than their team can topple with six batons
    - This mitigates the danger of a close advantage line
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

3. **Setting Up Doubles**
    - Inkasting deep near opponent's baseline can set up possible doubles with a base kubb
    - This is an advanced strategic consideration
    - [Source: Beginner's Guide to Inkasting](https://kubbon.com/2018/02/19/beginners-guide-inkasting/)

### The Driller Role

1. **Most Important Team Role**
    - The inkaster (driller) is one of the most important roles on a team
    - A good inkaster can help steal a game, but a bad one can almost guarantee defeat
    - [Source: New to Kubb: Team Roles](https://kubbon.com/2017/06/28/new-kubb-team-roles/)

2. **Versatility is Key**
    - Unlike other sports, kubb teams will need players who can play multiple roles
    - A well-rounded player is one who may be comfortable in any position
    - Even the best position player may have an off day, so everyone should practice everything
    - [Source: New to Kubb: Team Roles](https://kubbon.com/2017/06/28/new-kubb-team-roles/)

## General Strategy and Game Understanding

### Field Positioning

1. **Advantage Line Strategy**
    - When you have an advantage, you move your throwing line forward to the field kubb closest to the center line
    - This makes hitting baseline kubbs much easier
    - The advantage line only applies to throwing batons at kubbs, not to inkasting or attacking the king
    - [Source: Rules of Kubb — Kubb United](http://www.kubbunited.com/rules-of-kubb)
    - [Source: How to Play Kubb (Rules and Field Setup)](https://www.thebackyardbaron.com/how-to-play-kubb/)

2. **Risk of Close Advantage Line**
    - If you attempt to throw the kubbs close to the center line but fail to knock them all over, the other team has a very easy time
    - Think strategically about field kubb placement risk vs reward
    - [Source: Mastering Kubb: The Ultimate Guide to Winning Strategies](https://elakaioutdoor.com/blogs/lifestyle/mastering-kubb-the-ultimate-guide-to-winning-strategies)

3. **All Field Kubbs Must Fall**
    - It is imperative that all field kubbs are toppled because otherwise, opponents will be able to throw from a much closer point
    - This is one of the most critical strategic concepts in kubb
    - [Source: How to Play Kubb: A Beginner's Guide](https://freshhobby.com/how-to-play-kubb/)
    - [Source: How to Play Kubb with Simple Rules and Setup](https://tosscornhole.com/how-to-play-kubb/)

### King Shot Strategy

1. **King Shot Rules**
    - To win, you must knock down all kubbs on your opponent's side, then knock down the king
    - All king attacks happen from your original baseline, even if you had an advantage line
    - All throws at the king must be from behind the baseline
    - [Source: How to Be King of Kubb](https://www.instructables.com/How-to-be-King-of-Kubb/)
    - [Source: Rules of Kubb — Kubb United](http://www.kubbunited.com/rules-of-kubb)

2. **Premature King Hit Loses Game**
    - If the king is knocked down at any time before all kubbs are knocked down, that team loses immediately
    - Be careful not to accidentally hit the king when throwing at other kubbs
    - [Source: How to Play Kubb with Simple Rules and Setup](https://tosscornhole.com/how-to-play-kubb/)

3. **Opening King Toss**
    - To determine which team starts, a player from each team throws a stick as close to the king as possible without hitting it
    - The team with the closest throw begins
    - [Source: Go the Distance — With Kubb!](https://kubbon.com/2017/05/24/go-distance-kubb/)

### Mental Game and Psychology

1. **Psychological Warfare**
    - Players can psych out their opponent by talking smack (in good fun, of course)
    - Make them second guess their strategy
    - Keep up a cool, calm demeanor that screams confidence
    - [Source: Kubb Strategies and Tips](https://kangjiegardengame.com/kubb-strategies-and-tips-wanna-crush-your-opponents/)

2. **Maintain Composure**
    - Whether you're winning or losing, act like everything's going according to plan
    - The calmer you seem, the more pressure your opponent will feel
    - Before they know it, they'll be cracking under the weight of their own nerves
    - [Source: Kubb Strategies and Tips](https://kangjiegardengame.com/kubb-strategies-and-tips-wanna-crush-your-opponents/)

3. **Learn From Others**
    - Watch your opponent's technique and learn from good throwers
    - Notice their grip, release point, and follow through
    - Playing against different opponents exposes you to different tactics and strategies
    - [Source: Kubb Strategies and Tips](https://kangjiegardengame.com/kubb-strategies-and-tips-wanna-crush-your-opponents/)

4. **Strategic Thinking Over Force**
    - Kubb combines physical skill with cognitive and psychological dimensions
    - It's a game of wits as well as technique
    - Don't focus solely on power - consider strategic aspects
    - [Source: Kubb Strategies and Tips](https://kangjiegardengame.com/kubb-strategies-and-tips-wanna-crush-your-opponents/)

### Environmental Considerations

1. **Wind Adaptation**
    - U.S. tournament rules allow for a 45-degree angle from center to accommodate environmental conditions, especially wind
    - Wind is acknowledged as a significant environmental factor that affects gameplay
    - [Source: Rules of Kubb — Kubb United](http://www.kubbunited.com/rules-of-kubb)

2. **Surface Considerations**
    - Kubb can be played on grass, sand, snow, pebbles, or even ice
    - Hard surfaces like asphalt and concrete will wear out the logs faster and make them bounce unpredictably
    - Grass, sand, snow and pebbles are recommended surfaces
    - [Source: 6 Best Kubb Sets 2023 Reviews](https://www.gamequarium.com/best-kubb-set/)

3. **All-Weather Play**
    - Kubb tournaments can be played in rain, snow, or shine
    - The game is designed to be played in various weather conditions
    - [Source: Kubb - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kubb)

### Tournament and Competitive Play

1. **Tournament Format Understanding**
    - Throw-offs consist of each team inkasting three kubbs and hitting as many field kubbs and baseline kubbs as possible with six batons
    - Understanding tournament formats helps with practice planning
    - [Source: 2026 U.S. Championship](https://www.usakubb.org/2026-u-s-championship)

2. **Accuracy Over Risk-Taking**
    - It is more important to use less power and be accurate than to use power and completely miss
    - Leaving an extra kubb out occasionally that others who throw harder might get with the same throw can be offset by limiting complete misses
    - [Source: Blasting: Finesse Style](https://kubbon.com/2017/04/07/blasting-finesse-style/)

## Summary of Key Principles

### For 8-Meter Throws
- Lock your elbow, aim for 180-degree rotation
- Use a softer touch and smooth release
- Stand perpendicular to kubbs with feet shoulder-width apart
- Maintain consistent technique even when struggling
- Practice accuracy with corner pins and multi-distance drills

### For Blasting
- Assess pile type: power for tight piles, finesse for loose piles
- Hit the top corner of kubbs for maximum chaos
- Accuracy is more important than power
- Practice London Bridge drill and various distances
- Create baton movement after contact rather than just hard hits

### For Inkasting/Drilling
- "Crash the plane, don't land it" - nose down contact
- Spin like a football but with nose pointed down
- Find your spin happy medium - not too hard, not too soft
- Takes about 2 months of consistent practice to develop
- Cluster kubbs together for offensive advantage
- Consider deep throws when you have too many kubbs

### Overall Strategy
- All field kubbs must be cleared to prevent advantage line
- Watch and learn from other players
- Mental composure and consistency beat raw power
- Practice multiple roles and distances
- Make training harder than actual competition
